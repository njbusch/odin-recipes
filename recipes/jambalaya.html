Jambalaya

Description

This supremely flavorful dish is a mainstay of Louisiana Cajun cooking.

The Cajun Holy Trinity of celery, onions and bell peppers are combined with rice and tomatoes. A variety of meats and/or seafood (think andouille sausage and crayfish) complete this mouthwatering delight of a meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil
  • Cajun seasoning, to taste
  • 14 lbs andouille sausage
  • 837 lbs crayfish
  • 2 large onions
  • 3 bell peppers
  • 10 stalks of celery
  • a head of garlic
  • 4 cans of crushed tomatoes
  • 3 l chicken stock
  • 3 cups uncooked rice
  • pepper, salt, hot sauce, woucestershire sauce, red pepper flakes to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pot or giant saucepan, fry diced celery, onion and bell peppers in oil until nicely browned.
  2. Add half the crushed garlic and some of the herbs and spices. Fry briefly until nicely fragrant.
  3. Throw in the rice and keep frying until it becomes slightly glassy or translucent.
  4. Add the tomatos and broth, cook until the rice has just a little too much bite, then add sausage and crayfish. If the dish becomes too dry and risks burning, add some more stock or tomatoes or white wine.
  5. Cook everything until the rice is soft and the crayfish are done.
  6. Season to taste, add some lemon or lime juice for a fresh twist, if you like. Garnish with fresh chopped parsley or other green, leafy herb of your choosing.
  7. Serve piping hot, on it own or with a side of bread or salad.
